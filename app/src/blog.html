The Simple Answer to What is Digital Marketing

The answer to the question of what digital marketing is sounds simple. It is the digital version of marketing or in some cases it is marketing. Another definition is the use of digital media to perform online marketing and promotion such as SEO. Those definitions fail to describe anything. Let's see if we can do better.

Digital marketing is communicating to an audience using electronic devices that are measurable to achieve a desired result. (h2 – higher emphasis like blockquote as well)

A problem with definitions of digital marketing is listing specific tactics or channels. While examples are helpful it makes the definition too specific. With digital marketing in particular, change is going to happen. We shouldn't keep updating definitions each year. Constant change in definitions causes the confusion we are trying to avoid.

Is there a difference between marketing and digital marketing? Yes. Digital marketing is a subset of marketing. Digital marketing uses devices for message delivery. You can also do more advanced measurement with digital marketing.

Sometimes it helps to know what something is not. Marketing includes what I will term disconnected communication (it is more complex than that, but not helpful here). Think of traditional billboards, mailers, ads in magazines, etc. These are static and more difficult to track mediums. Digital marketing, as you can see, is not all marketing. Our definition does not assume a specific action. Going back to our definition let's break it down so it becomes simple. You should be able to evaluate your digital marketing against this.
